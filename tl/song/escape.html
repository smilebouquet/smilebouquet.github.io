  1. the actual word used here is 粟立つ (awadatsu), which means "to get goosebumps​". i chose to translate this as "bone-chilling" because 粟立つ appears to be more closely linked to coldness or fear (compared to in english where "getting chills/goosebumps" can be interpretted positively or negatively).
  2. 取り戻す literally means “to take you back”.
  3. 打ち解ける in this case means “to be more relaxed/to lower your guard”.
