  1. the second-person pronoun 貴女 (anata) is used here! 'anata' is a fairly neutral pronoun when used in creative writing, with the kanji 女 ('woman') aiding in the vision of 'you' being a lady or princess. it's a common pronoun to see in knights songs—silent oath, grateful allegiance, and promise swords all use 貴女.
  2. 語らう (katarau) means "to talk (i.e. have a conversation with someone)", but it can also mean "to pledge" or "to exchange wedding vows".
  3. this line can be read two different ways: "i want our love to be difficult to turn into a fairytale", or "i want our love to continue beyond the ending of a fairytale".
  4. 雄弁 (yuuben) means "eloquent", and is an adjective that is typically used to describe speech. this line is likely playing with the japanese saying 目は口ほどに物を言う (me wa kuchi hodo ni mono wo iu lit. "the eyes say as much as the mouth"), which is similar in definition to the english saying "eyes are a window of the soul".
